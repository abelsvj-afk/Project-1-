# Eldoria: Canon NPC Roster

## 1. The Scavenger Underground (Grounded Knowledge)
### **Kaelen** (The "Everyman")
- **Role**: Initial guide. A regular scavenger trying to survive.
- **Nuance**: He knows nothing of the "Deep Lore." He knows where to find scrap and which guards are easy to bribe. His "hints" are based on street-level observation (e.g., "The Watch takes more spirits than they report").
- **Dynamic**: Loyal to survival. If the player makes him rich, he's a friend. If the player makes him a target, he vanishes.

### **Torin** (The Warrens Architect)
- **Role**: A master tinkerer who built the ventilation-city beneath the Outpost.
- **Goal**: To build a self-sustaining grid independent of the Syndicate.
- **Connection**: He is the first person who will recognize the player's "Modern Blueprints" as revolutionary rather than magical.

## 2. The Iron Watch (The Syndicate/Order)
### **Inquisitor Vane** (The Architect of the Harvest)
- **Role**: Primary antagonist (High Status Tier).
- **Goal**: Triggering a controlled "Second Fall" to reshape reality.
- **Nuance**: He believes he is saving the world from entropy. He sees the player as a "lost tool" of the original collapse.

### **Officer Lyra** (The Conflict of Duty)
- **Role**: A field commander who carries out Vane's orders.
- **Nuance**: She witnesses the cruelty of the "Harvest" first-hand. She is the potential defector or the "Honorable Enemy" who respects the player's prowess.

## 3. The Adept Spires (The Spiritual/Chaos)
### **The Silent Scribe (Malakor)**
- **Role**: An ancient Adept who remembers the actual day of the Fall.
- **Goal**: Total integration of spirits and humans, regardless of the physical cost (Transhumanism).
- **Connection**: He understands the "Fallen Spirit" in the player's chest better than anyone, but he wants to study it—destructively.

# The Core Narrative Gameplay Loop

1. **Discovery (The Modern Perspective)**:
   - The player encounters a problem (e.g., a failing steam engine, a Syndicate static-shield).
   - Through exploration or high `Logic` stats, the player "recollects" an Earth-based scientific principle (Faraday Cages, Pressure Regulators).

2. **Action (The Catalyst)**:
   - The player applies this "New Blueprint" to the world.
   - This disrupts the status quo (e.g., the Syndicate's grid fails in one sector).

3. **Reaction (The Spider-Web)**:
   - Factions react dynamically. The Syndicate sends Inquisitors (Vane) to investigate the "Anomaly."
   - The Scavengers (Torin) see the player as a messiah of technology.
   - The Adepts (Malakor) fear the "Anchoring" of their chaotic magic.

4. **Evolution (The Morph)**:
   - These actions build "Resonance Stability." 
   - The Echo-Anchor in the chest matures, eventually "Discovering" the hidden skill that allows the player to fundamentally change the map's physics.
