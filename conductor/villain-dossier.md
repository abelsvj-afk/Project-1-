# Adversary Dossier: Director-General Elias Thorne

## Core Identity
**Name:** Director-General Elias Thorne
**Faction:** The Iron Syndicate (High Command)
**The Possession:** Vessel for "The Blighted Sovereign" (an ancient, vengeful spirit from the Fall of Aetheria).
**Alignment:** Corrupt / Evil (with twisted, rigid Logic).

## Visual Description (Arcane-Industrial Aesthetic)
Thorne presents a terrifying hybridization of rigid bureaucracy and corrupted magic. 
*   **The Uniform:** He wears the pristine, sharp-tailored greatcoat of the Iron Syndicate High Command—heavy black leather, polished brass buttons, and reinforced iron epaulets.
*   **The Corruption:** The left side of his body is being consumed by a jagged, obsidian-like crystalline growth (the physical manifestation of the blighted spirit). 
*   **The Containment:** To prevent the spirit from fully taking over, his entire left arm and neck are encased in a massive, heavy brass "Containment Gauntlet." It violently hisses steam, leaking a sickly, static green light as it forcibly suppresses the spirit's energy through sheer physical pressure and cooling vents.
*   **The Eyes:** His right eye is human, cold, and calculating. His left eye is completely replaced by a glowing green fracture.

## Personality & Psychology
Thorne is a man fighting a war on two fronts: one against the lawlessness of Eldoria, and one inside his own mind.
*   **The Bureaucrat:** He is cold, ruthlessly efficient, and views organic life (and spirits) as resources to be extracted. He believes he is the only one strong enough to bring "Order" to the world.
*   **The Sovereign:** The spirit inside him is ancient, arrogant, and demands vengeance against the world that survived the Fall. 
*   **The Synthesis:** Thorne does not view the possession as a curse. He views it as a *weapon* he has successfully subjugated. He uses the spirit's immense power to fuel his political rise, while the spirit slowly twists Thorne's definition of "Order" into "Annihilation." When he speaks, he occasionally slips into an archaic, booming voice, followed by the violent hissing of his containment suit compensating for the surge in power.

## Motivations & Goals
1.  **Total Industrialization:** Wipe out the Adepts and their "primitive" worship of spirits.
2.  **The Harvest:** Drain the Borderlands (and eventually the Nexus) of all raw resonance to fuel a massive, world-altering piece of technology (perhaps a weapon to strike back at the shattered sky, or to make himself a living god).
3.  **Containment:** Find the "Null Anomaly" (the Player). Thorne realizes his containment suit is failing; he needs the Player's unique "Echo-Anchor" ability to permanently bind the Sovereign's power to his will without dying.

## QBN Systemic Triggers (How he affects the game)
*   **Syndicate Menace:** As the player gains power, Thorne's attention (a hidden `thorne_awareness` stat) increases.
*   **Morphing Text:** When Thorne is near, procedural text should emphasize the smell of *ozone, burning copper, and rotting flesh*, overriding normal environmental descriptions.
*   **Combat:** Thorne is a late-game boss who utilizes both physical Iron Syndicate artillery (requiring Balance management) and devastating psychic/blighted attacks (requiring Equilibrium management).
