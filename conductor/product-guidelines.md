
# Eldoria: Product Guidelines

## 1. Prose & Tone
The narrative prose must be highly adaptive, shifting its tone to reflect the current circumstances of the story and the world. 
- During moments of tension or high "menace," the language should become terse and action-oriented. 
- During exploration of safe zones, it should expand into evocative, immersive descriptions.
- The overarching rule is **Contextual Resonance**: The text must always feel appropriate to the player's immediate situation, companions, and physical location.

## 2. User Experience (UX) Philosophy
The interface must prioritize clarity and immersion through two core principles:
- **Persistent Visibility**: Critical interactive elements (like the "Your Move" choices menu) must remain sticky and visible at all times, anchoring the player to the game state without forcing them to scroll back.
- **Tactile Separation**: Distinct game states (Narrative exploration vs. Achaea-style Combat) must have dedicated, visually separated interfaces to prevent cognitive overload.

## 3. Onboarding & Progression
Eldoria embraces the "Elden Ring" philosophy of learning through exploration and consequence, but balances it for the text medium:
- **Hybrid Gating**: The game leans heavily into *Trial by Fire*, placing the player in a dangerous world immediately. However, complex systemic mechanics (like the Echo-Anchor or advanced combat tactics) are introduced via *Contextual Gating*—revealed only when the player encounters a scenario that demands them.
- **Optional Structure**: Minimal *Structured Tutorials* (via lore fragments or NPC hints) may be scattered throughout the early game for players who seek them out, but they are never forced.
