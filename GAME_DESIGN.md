# Eldoria: Game Design & Discovery

This document serves as the repository for narrative, mechanical, and world-building concepts as they are developed.

## Core Pillar: Magic-Tech Exploitation
**The Hook:** Magic in this world is not just a mystical force; it is a resource to be exploited through created technology. This leads to unique armor, various weapons, and a systemic evolution of the player character.

### Discovery Questions
1. **The Source of Magic:** Is magic a natural force (like air) that people are now "trapping" in machines, or is it something extracted from living creatures (which makes the tech "evil" by default)?
2. **The "Tech" Aesthetic:** When you think of this tech, is it **Steampunk** (gears, steam, clunky brass), **Cyberpunk-Fantasy** (glowing runes, wires, sleek metal), or **Arcane-Industrial** (huge glowing pipes, floating crystals held by iron clamps)?
3. **The Skill Tree Concept:** Do you want a "Web" where everything is connected (like Path of Exile), or distinct "Paths" where if you go too far into Tech, you lose the ability to use natural Magic?
4. **The "Siphon" System:** How does the player interact with magic-tech? Slots in gear for captured spirits/mana crystals, or something more integrated?
5. **The Cost of Exploitation:** Does using "Magic-Tech" have a visible cost on the world or the user's "Purity/Corruption"? (e.g., environmental decay, "cyborg-adjacent" physical morphing).

## Narrative Pillars
- **Tone:** To be decided (Whimsical, Grimdark, or Industrial).
- **Conflict:** To be decided (Power Vacuum, Global Corruption, or Personal Quest).
- **Canon NPCs:** To be decided (Mentors, Rivals, Subjective Factions).

## Planned Mechanical Depth
- **Modular Inventory:** Focus on "Systemic Gear" where components (hilts, cores, fuel) can be swapped.
- **Involved Skill Tree:** A deep, multi-path system reflecting the player's alignment with nature or technology.
- **Dynamic Text Generation:** Descriptions that shift based on the player's mechanical literacy and moral alignment.
